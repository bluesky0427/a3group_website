## 5. Company Philosophy

**Technology begins with people.**

At A3 Technology Group, we started in IT and software, and that engineering foundation still runs through everything we do. But we understand technology in its fullest sense. Technology is not only software or machines — it begins with human knowledge, skill, tools, systems, and cooperation. Every advance in human history has come from people learning to do something well and then combining that ability with the work of others.

**Everyone has skills.**

Scientists, doctors, engineers, and mathematicians hold valuable expertise — but they are not the only ones. Builders, technicians, drivers, farmers, service workers, creators, and business owners all carry real knowledge and ability. Some people have skills they have not yet discovered, simply because they have not had the right opportunity, environment, or guidance to use them.

**Division of labor creates new value.**

Human society advances because people specialize. When different specialties are brought together deliberately, they produce results no individual could imagine or build alone. This is the engine of progress, and it is the principle at the center of our work.

**Opportunity reveals potential.**

Skill without opportunity stays hidden. Our role is to create the conditions — the connections, the structure, and the technology — where ability becomes real, measurable value.

**A3 Technology Group connects people, skills, and technology to create value.**

We bring together technology and talent across industries, match capability to opportunity, and turn it into products, services, and ventures that work in the real world. That is how we create value — for our clients, our partners, and the people we work with.

## 9. Brand Tone & Design Direction

> **This is the identity actually implemented on the live site.** The palette and type below match the built Next.js codebase (`tailwind.config.ts`), so the document and the website stay in sync.

- **Tone:** Professional, confident, modern, and clear. Inspiring but grounded — never charity-like, never exaggerated. Speaks to business value first, with philosophy as the backbone.
- **Color palette — deep navy base with a refined gold accent:**
  - **Navy** (primary): `#0B2545` (DEFAULT) deepening to `#071A33` (900) and lightening to `#EAF0F7` (50). Carries authority, trust, and the technology foundation. Used for hero backgrounds, headings, and primary buttons.
  - **Gold** (accent): `#C8A04D` (DEFAULT), `#DDBE7E` (light), `#A9863A` (dark). Used *sparingly* — eyebrows, icons, key highlights, the "human / opportunity" thread. Gold is the warmth against the navy.
  - **Ink** (text): `#1F2933` (body), `#52606D` (light), `#7B8794` (muted).
  - **Sage** (optional alternate accent): `#3F7D6E` / `#6BA294` — available for variety, used minimally.
  - **Neutrals:** white and the navy-50 tint (`#EAF0F7`) for section backgrounds.
- **Typography — sans for clarity, serif for gravitas:**
  - **Headings & UI:** *Inter* (semibold/bold) — clean, modern, highly readable.
  - **Editorial accents & pull-quotes:** *Playfair Display* (serif) — used for philosophy quotes and statement numbers to add credibility and a considered, established feel.
  - No script or decorative fonts.
- **Layout:** Spacious and grid-based. Generous white space, clear section breaks, large readable headlines, strong visual hierarchy. Card grids for services/divisions. Sticky top navigation. Subtle `fade-up` / `fade-in` entrance animations and soft shadows (`shadow-card`, `shadow-soft`) for depth without noise.
- **Imagery:** Real, professional photography over generic stock where possible — people of different industries and backgrounds collaborating; technology in real working contexts; clean abstract geometric accents (the site currently uses a tasteful grid/blur motif in the hero instead of stock photos, which keeps it fast and timeless). Avoid clichés (handshakes over globes, glowing brains).
- **Overall brand feeling:** A serious, established technology company with a forward-looking, human-centered vision — credible to enterprise clients, inviting to collaborators, and inspiring to individuals.

## 9b. Logo & the Meaning of "A3"

The name should feel deliberate, not decorative. A clear story behind "A3" makes the brand memorable and gives sales/partnership conversations an easy hook.

**Recommended meaning — the "three A's" the company stands for:**

> **A3 = Ability · Alliance · Achievement**
> - **Ability** — everyone holds valuable skill and potential (the human foundation).
> - **Alliance** — cross-industry collaboration and the division of labor (skills combined).
> - **Achievement** — real, practical value and opportunity created (the result).

This maps cleanly onto your philosophy: *ability → alliance → achievement* is the same arc as *skill → collaboration → value*. It is concrete, business-credible, and easy to explain in one line on the About page.

*Alternative readings you could adopt instead, if you prefer:* "Three A's of growth — **Aspire, Align, Advance**," or a literal origin ("A-cubed / A³ — skill multiplied by collaboration multiplied by opportunity"). Pick one and use it consistently.

**Logo direction:**
- **Wordmark:** "A3" set in a strong geometric sans (Inter or a slightly more distinctive geometric like Sora/Sole), with "TECHNOLOGY GROUP" in spaced uppercase beneath or beside it.
- **Monogram / mark:** treat the **"3"** as the hero element — three short horizontal strokes or three converging lines can represent the three A's and the idea of separate skills meeting. A subtle gold accent stroke on an otherwise navy mark ties it to the palette.
- **The "³" idea:** rendering it as **A³** (superscript) is a clever nod to "multiplication / exponential value from combined skills." Works well as a favicon and in dense layouts. The current site favicon (`src/app/icon.svg`) is the place to apply this.
- **Usage:** navy mark on light backgrounds; white (or white + gold) mark on the navy hero. Keep clear space around it; avoid gradients or 3D effects — flat and confident.
- **Favicon/app icon:** the simplified **A³** monogram in navy/gold already has a home at `src/app/icon.svg` and the web manifest (`src/app/manifest.ts`).

## 13. Deployment Guide (Vercel)

The site is a standard Next.js 14 app and is built to deploy on **Vercel** (the platform behind Next.js) with zero extra configuration. Estimated time: ~15 minutes plus DNS propagation.

### Step 1 — Put the code in Git
The project isn't a git repo yet. Initialise it and push to GitHub (recommended for automatic deploys):
```bash
git init
git add .
git commit -m "Initial commit: A3 Technology Group website"
# create an empty repo on github.com first, then:
git remote add origin https://github.com/<your-account>/a3-technology-group.git
git branch -M main
git push -u origin main
```
> `.gitignore` already excludes `node_modules`, `.next`, and `.env.local`, so secrets won't be committed.

### Step 2 — Import into Vercel
1. Sign up / log in at **vercel.com** (use your GitHub account).
2. **Add New → Project → Import** your `a3-technology-group` repo.
3. Vercel auto-detects Next.js — framework, build command (`next build`), and output are set for you. Just click **Deploy**.

*(No GitHub? Alternative: install the Vercel CLI with `npm i -g vercel`, run `vercel` in the project folder, and follow the prompts.)*

### Step 3 — Add environment variables
In **Project → Settings → Environment Variables**, add the same keys from `.env.example` (set them for **Production**, and Preview if you want):

| Key | Value |
|---|---|
| `RESEND_API_KEY` | your real Resend API key (`re_...`) |
| `CONTACT_TO_EMAIL` | `info@a3technologygroup.com` |
| `CONTACT_FROM_EMAIL` | `no-reply@a3technologygroup.com` |
| `CONTACT_THANK_YOU_FROM_EMAIL` | `no-reply@a3technologygroup.com` |
| `NEXT_PUBLIC_SITE_URL` | `https://a3technologygroup.com` |

Then **redeploy** so the new variables take effect.

### Step 4 — Connect the domain `a3technologygroup.com`
1. In **Project → Settings → Domains**, add `a3technologygroup.com` **and** `www.a3technologygroup.com`.
2. Vercel shows the DNS records to add at your domain registrar:
   - Apex (`a3technologygroup.com`) → an **A record** to Vercel's IP (`76.76.21.21`), or follow Vercel's "nameservers" option.
   - `www` → a **CNAME** to `cname.vercel-dns.com`.
3. ⚠️ **Do not touch your existing email DNS.** Leave the **MX, SPF, DKIM, and DMARC** records (Google Workspace) exactly as they are — you're only adding website records (A/CNAME) and the separate Resend *sending* records. Email and website DNS live side by side.
4. SSL/HTTPS is issued automatically by Vercel once DNS resolves (can take a few minutes to a couple of hours).

### Step 5 — Verify Resend sending domain (for the contact form)
1. In the **Resend dashboard**, add and verify `a3technologygroup.com` as a sending domain.
2. Resend gives you a **DKIM CNAME** and an **SPF TXT** record (usually on a `send.` subdomain). Add those at your registrar — again, *alongside* your existing Google records, not replacing them.
3. Once verified, the form's notification email and the automatic thank-you email will send live.

### Step 6 — Post-launch checks
- Visit the live URL; click through all five pages.
- Submit the contact form with a real email and confirm both the internal notification and the thank-you email arrive.
- Check `https://a3technologygroup.com/sitemap.xml` and `/robots.txt` resolve.
- (Optional) Submit the sitemap in **Google Search Console** to start indexing.

### Ongoing
With GitHub connected, every `git push` to `main` auto-deploys to production, and pull requests get preview URLs. No manual build/upload needed.
